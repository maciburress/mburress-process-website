Research

Before creating the website, I dove into deep primary and secondary researches as well as interviewing members of the organization to get a better understanding of what their goals and purposes are.

Primary Research

Client Website Research

Beginning the primary research, visiting the client's website is step one in finding key insights of the organization. The website provides a lot of useful information on what they do and how one can become a member.

General Information: The daily activity at a Clubhouse is similar to any work place, consisting of 8-hour days, Monday through Friday. Members work at the clubhouse developing skills both mentally and professionally that they can use for future living.

New Day Services: Employees provide members with social and vocational skills, administrative services, such as word processing, data entry, billing, and other responsibilites, such as lawn care, cleaning, snack bar operations, and food services. These responsibilites provide members with a variety of work experiences for future jobs.

Secondary Research

Clubhouses are used specifically for mental illness individuals who want and seek help. Individuals who join clubhouses are referred to as “members.” 1. The Clubhouse model has been proved to be an effective way to help mentally ill people to transition into everyday life.

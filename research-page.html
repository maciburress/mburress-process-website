Research

Before creating the website, I dove into deep primary and secondary researches as well as interviewing members of the organization to get a better understanding of what their goals and purposes are.

Primary Research

New Day provides members with social and vocational skills to successfully adjust to community life. Members volunteer in one of three different areas at the center: administrative services, such as word processing, data entry and billing; membership services, such as lawn care, cleaning and snack bar operations; and food services, such as meal planning and preparation and shopping for groceries. The center also helps members with transitional employment at local businesses. The kitchen allows members to prepare healthier meals from scratch without having to use prepackaged and processed items A need base programs Music, art, games, activities The daily activity at a Clubhouse is similar to any work place, consisting of 8-hour days Monday through Friday. Members and staff work side-by-side, performing work for the Clubhouse. Members are encouraged to participate as they are ready and quickly become relied upon. Typical daily work includes: planning and preparing lunch for Clubhouse colleagues. maintaining attendance and accounting records for billing purposes. communications about Clubhouse events, employment celebrations, and membership tours. Long term goal is for members to live independently in the community by becoming self-sufficient individuals.
